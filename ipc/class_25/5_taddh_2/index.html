    ### *a (from, belonging to)
    gotama + *a gotama from family of Gotama
    bharadvāja + *a bhāradvāja from family of Bharadvāja
    jambu + nadī + *a jambonada from Jambu River
    janapada + *a jānapada from countryside, rural
    magadha + *a māgadha from Magadha
    nagara + *a nāgara from a city, citizen
    tila + *a tela from sesame seed, oil
    uras + *a orasa from breast, natural
    vasudeva + *a vāsudeva from God Vishnu

    ### *a (related to, regarding, connected with)
    hima + anta + *a hemanta related to cold end, winter
    kumāra + *a komāra related to youth, youthful
    sarīra + *a sārīra related to the body, physical
    usabha + *a āsabha related to the head bull
    vyaggha + *a veyaggha related to tigers

    ### *a (studying, knowing)

    ### *ika (agent nouns)

    In traditional grammars, these fall under many categories, such as living-in, working-with, going by, crossing-with, but they are all just agent nouns.
    abbhokāsa + *ika abbhokāsika open air ascetic
    addhā + *ika addhika traveler
    ārāma + *ika ārāmika monastery worker
    arañña + *ika āraññika forest dweller
    bhatta + *ika bhattika eater
    dvāra + *ika dovārika door-keeper, guard
    gāma + *ika gāmika villager
    kathā + *ika kathika story-teller, narrator
    magadha + *ika māgadhika lives in Magadha
    navā + *ika nāvika sailor
    ratha + *ika rathika charioteer
    rukkhamūla + *ika rukkhamūlika tree dweller
    sakaṭa + *ika sākaṭika carter
    sūkara + *ika sūkarika pig butcher

    ### ima (belonging to, most)
    belonging to the ...
    anta + ima antima end > final
    heṭṭḥā + ima heṭṭhima lower > lowest
    majjha + ima majjhima middle > medium
    ora + ima orima near shore > closest
    parā + ima pārima far shore > furthest
    pura + ima purima before, east > earlier, eastern
    pacchā + ima pacchima after, west > last, western
    paccanta + ima paccantima far end > border, frontier
    ***
