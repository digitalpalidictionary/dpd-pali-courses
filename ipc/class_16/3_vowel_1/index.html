    Elision of a Vowel

    Vowel elision is of two types, forward and backwards elision.

    Think of a Pac-Man going forward, it eats the letter in front of it. This is called forward elision.

    Think of a Pac-Man going backwards, it eats the letter behind it. This is called backwards elision, and very common in Pāli.

    Possibility #1

    a vowel before another vowel is sometimes elided

    ajjauposatho > ajjuposatho

    au > u

    This means:

    when a word ends in a,

    and the next word starts with u,

    the a can be elided,

    and only the u will remain.

    In Kaccāyana this is called (12) sarā sare lopaṃ
