# Possibility \#9
***
When **ṃ** is followed by **a vowel**, the vowel may be **elided**.
***
The **ṃ** then may change according to **Possibility \#2**
***
e.g. abhinanduṃ + **i**ti
***
\> abhinandu**ṃ** + (i)ti
***
\> abhinandunti
***
In Kaccāyana this is called (40) **paro vā saro** and
***
(31) **vagg’antaṃ vā vagge.**
***
**ṃ + vowel \> elision + nasal**
| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| abhinandu**ṃ**                   | **i**ti | abhinandu**n**ti | they appreciated    |
| aha**ṃ**                         | **i**ti | aha**n**ti       | “I”                 |
| appa**ṃ**                        | **a**pi | appa**m**pi      | even a little       |
| aya**ṃ**                         | **a**pi | aya**m**pi       | even this           |
| dutiya**ṃ**                      | **a**pi | dutiya**m**pi    | for the second time |
| eva**ṃ**                         | **a**pi | eva**m**pi       | even thus           |
| ka**ṃ**                          | **a**si | ka**ṃ**si        | who you are?        |
| cakka**ṃ**                       | **i**va | cakka**ṃ**va     | like a wheel        |
| rukka**ṃ**                       | **i**va | rukkha**ṃ**va    | like a tree         |
***
There are hundreds of examples with **iva,** with the meaning of a **like** or **as**. These are just from the Dhammapada:
***
cakkaṃva rukkhaṃva selaṃva seṭṭhaṃva abalassaṃva niratthaṃva khīraṃva nidhīnaṃva paṭivātaṃva ñātīnaṃva nakkhattapathaṃva bhantaṃva kaliṃva tulaṃva sāradikaṃva phalamicchaṃva abhivaṭṭhaṃva naḷaṃva bhadraṃva aggihuttaṃva candaṃva
***
**Counter-examples:**
***
ahaṃ + eva \> ahameva
***
etaṃ + ahosi \> etadahosi
***
