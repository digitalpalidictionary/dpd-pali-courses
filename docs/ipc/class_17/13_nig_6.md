# Possibility \#6
***
When **ṃ** is followed by a vowel, it can become **m** or **d**
***
e.g. ya**ṃ** + **ā**hu \> ya**m**āhu
***
eta**ṃ** + **a**voca \> eta**d**avoca
***
In Kaccāyana this is called (34) **madā sare.**
***
**ṃ + vowel \> m**
| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| ta**ṃ**            | **a**haṃ   | ta**m**ahaṃ   | that I            |
| eta**ṃ**           | **a**haṃ   | eta**m**ahaṃ  | this I            |
| ta**ṃ**            | **a**tthaṃ | ta**m**atthaṃ | that purpose      |
| ya**ṃ**            | **ā**hu    | ya**m**āhu    | whatever they say |
| ki**ṃ**            | **a**ssa   | ki**m**assa   | what is his?      |
***
**ṃ + vowel \> d**
| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| eta**ṃ**           | **a**voca | eta**d**avoca      | said this       |
| ya**ṃ**            | **i**daṃ  | ya**d**idaṃ        | whichever this  |
| eta**ṃ**           | **e**va   | eta**d**eva        | just this       |
| yaṃ ya**ṃ**        | **e**va   | ya**ññ**a**d**eva  | whatever at all |
***
this is influenced by the Sanskrit **yad tad etad** and **tam** **etam** etc
***
**Counter-examples:**
***
ma**ṃ a**vadhi
***
ma**ṃ a**hāsi
***
