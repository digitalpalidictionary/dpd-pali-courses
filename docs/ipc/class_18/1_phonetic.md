# Phonetic Changes
***
There are six types of phonetic changes that happen in Sandhi. Some of them you have already seen, but now you can put a name to them.
***
1.  Assimilation
***
2.  Adaption
***
3.  Metathesis
***
4.  Epenthesis (insertion)
***
5.  Aspiration
***
6.  Simplification
***
1.  **Assimilation** happens when two consonants come into contact with each other. There are three types
***
1.  **Progressive Assimilation**
***
√da**m + y**a \> da**mm**a (ptp. to be tamed)
***
√bu**dh + t**a \> bu**dht**a \> bu**ddh**a (pp. known)
***
1.  **Regressive Assimilation**
***
√ta**p + t**a \> ta**tt**a (pp. heated)
***
ud + √pa**d + n**a \> uppa**nn**a (pp. arisen)
***
1.  **Reciprocal Assimilation (mutual)**
***
√la**bh + t**a \> la**bht**a \> la**ddh**a (pp. obtained)
***
√jī**r + n**a \> ji**ṇṇ**a (adj. old)
***
(mainly for ease in pronunciation)
***
1.  **Adaption** occurs when a consonant changes from the influence of another consonant. We have seen this with nasal consonants
***
sa**ṃ + t**iṭṭhati \> sa**nt**iṭṭhati
***
sa**ṃ + j**ānāti \> sa**ñj**ānāti
***
2.  **Metathesis** is the interchange of two consonants
***
ava + √ru**h + y**a \> oru**hy**a \> oru**yh**a (ger. descending)
***
na + **pa** + √**cal** + a \> a**pac**ala \> a**cap**ala (adj. not shaking)
***
kareyya \> ka**r**i**y**ā \> ka**y**i**r**ā (should do)
***
3.  **Epenthesis** is the **insertion** of a letter in the middle of a word.
***
śrī (sk) \> s**i**rī (f. good luck)
***
hrī (sk) \> h**i**rī (f. conscience)
***
√nhā + ta \> n**a**hāta (pp. bathed)
***
pa + √hā + tvāna + paha**n**tvāna (abs. having given up)
***
4.  **Aspiration** an -h- is added
***
√as + ti \> atti \> att**h**i (pr. there is)
***
√is + ta \> iṭṭa \> iṭṭ**h**a (pp. wished, desired)
***
5.  **Simplification** involves reducing three consonants in a group to two, or two to one.
***
√ka**r + t**uṃ \> k**att**uṃ \> k**āt**uṃ
***
du**kkh**a \> du**kh**a
***
