# Kahāpaṇāna![](/assets/images/kahapana.png)
***
**Kahāpaṇānas from Kosala. Circa 525-465 BC.**
***
Average diameter 25mm, average weight 2.70 g. Each piece with a variety of separate punch-marks applied to both sides.
***
kahāpaṇāna = 4 pāda
***
1 pāda = 5 māsaka
***
**Fun Indo-European Etymology Story[^25]**

[^25]: Please understand that this is meant as a humorous element.
***
The Sanskrit word for kahāpaṇāna is *kārṣāpaṇa*.
***
The coins were called *kārṣāpaṇa* because they weighed one *karṣa* each, a weight measured in cowrie shells.
***
The English word, "Cash", seems similar to the Sanskrit word, *karṣa*.
